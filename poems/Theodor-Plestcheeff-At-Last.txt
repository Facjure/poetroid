No word,—not even a sigh, my darling!
  Together now the silence keeping;
In truth as over some grave stone leaning
  The silent willows low are weeping,
And drooping over it so are reading—
  I read in your tired heart at last,
That days of happiness existed,
  And that this happiness is past.
